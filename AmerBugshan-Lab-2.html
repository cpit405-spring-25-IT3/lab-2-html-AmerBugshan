The Sun is the center of our solar system, providing energy for life on Earth. It emits light and heat due to hydrogen2 fusion reactions in its core. Its temperature reaches about 5,500oC on the surface, making it a powerful star.

Water, with the formula H2O, is essential for survival. It has a unique property of expanding when frozen, which helps sustain life. Its boiling point is 100oC, making it suitable for varied environments. The oceans cover most of the Earth's surface.

Plants require photosynthesis to convert sunlight into energy. This process uses CO2 and H2O to produce glucose and oxygen. Chlorophyll, a green pigment, plays a key role. The reaction can be summarized as 6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2. Plants form the base of the food chain.

The speed of light is a universal constant, symbolized by c. It measures approximately 3 x 108 m/s in a vacuum. Light’s properties, such as wavelength (λ) and frequency (f), are related by c = fλ. This is crucial for understanding modern physics.

DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, carries genetic information. It is structured as a double helix, with base pairs like A-T and G-C. RNA differs by having uracil (U) instead of thymine. DNAreplication is an essential process for life. It is often called the blueprint of life.
